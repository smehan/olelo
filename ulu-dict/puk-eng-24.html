X

x

No Hawaiian term.

xerox

Pepa kope.

X-ray

Nānā i loko o ke kino.

X-ray machine

Mīkini nānā iā loko o ke kanaka.

Y

y

No Hawaiian term.

yacht

Moku peʻa.

yak

Iaka.

yam

Uhi (Oceanic; for various kinds, see uhi and entries that follow it; not to be confused with sweet Potato). Also: pālau, ulehihi, hoʻonohonoho, lehua, piʻa, poni, laha, niʻihau, ʻālela, uhi ʻālela. Bitter yam, hoi; piʻoi (Kauaʻi; Cf. ʻalaʻala). Yam pudding, piele.

yard

1. Unit of measure. Iā, iwilei, pio.

2. Spar. Iā.

3. Enclosure. Pā. House yard, pā hale.

yardstick

Lāʻau ana.

yarn

1. Spun material. Pāniani, lopi huluhulu.

2. Story. Kole, moʻolelo.

yawn

Hāmama, hoʻohāmama.

yaws

See ulcer.

ye

ʻOlua, ʻoukou.

year

Makahiki. New Year, Makahiki Hou. Leap year, makahiki lele ʻoi, makahiki lā keu. Happy New Year, Hauʻoli Makahiki Hou.

yearling

Pipi kolo (beef).

yearly

Makahiki, kūmakahiki, i kēlā me kēia makahiki.

yearn

Ake,ʻiʻini, nipo, ninipo, niponipo, liʻa, haʻe, ʻīloli, hoʻīloli.

yeast

Hoʻohū, hū.

yell

ʻUwā, hoʻōho; ʻea (rare).

yellow

Melemele, lena, ʻōlena, ʻōlenalena, lenalena. Also: mālena, pualena, kamalena, mele, memele, pua hau; pala (as leaves). Pale yellow, hālena, pia, hēpia, nāʻū. Golden yellow, pala luhiehu. Reddish-yellow, pala ʻehu. To begin to turn yellow, hāʻama.

yellowfin tuna

ʻAhi.

yellowish

Hālenalena, hālena, lenalena, nenanena, hālelo, ʻano melemele. Also: lena, lelo, pōlena, mea; kēhue (as soil).

yellow jacket

Hopeʻō.

yellow red

ʻUla lena.

Yellow Sea

Kai Melemele.

yellowtail

Same as amberjack

yelp

ʻAlalā, ʻalalī.

yes

ʻAe, ʻē; ō (giving permission); eō (in answer to a call by name, or to acknowledge a name chant in oneʻs honor); ʻū (signifying listening).

yesterday

Nehinei, inehinei. Day before yesterday, nehinei ā ia lā aku.

yet

1. Still. Naʻe. Not yet, koe, ʻaʻole.

2. See but.

yield

1. To produce. Hua, hoʻohua; hua loaʻa.

2. To agree. ʻAe, kuʻu.

ylang-ylang

Lanalana.

yodel

Hoʻōeoe.

yoke

Kua (of a holokū). Also: ʻauamo, lei. To yoke or harness together, kaulua. Ox yoke, kua pipi, lei pipi.

yokefellow

Hoa paʻahana.

yolk

Kauō melemele (of an egg).

yonder

ʻŌ, i ʻō, maʻō.

Yorktown

Iōkakaona, Iokataona.

you

ʻOe (singular); ʻolua (dual); ʻoukou (plural); kēnā; iū (rare). Vocative, ē, ʻea. You here (sometimes affectionate), eia nei, ei nei. You over there, eia ala, eia lā. For, by you, nāu. To, toward, for you, nou, i ou, iā ʻoe.

young

ʻŌpio, ʻōpiopio. Also: kiokio; kaekae (as an attractive woman); moʻo, moʻomoʻo, ʻalapuʻu; ʻouo (of animals); pua, ʻōhua (of certain fish); ʻōilo (as of ʻōʻio or eel). Young people, poʻeʻōpiopio, poʻe uʻi.

younger

Muli, muli iho, hope, ʻōpio. Younger branch of family, muʻo.

youngest

Hope loa, muli loa.

Young Peopleʻs Christian Endeavor Society

Hōʻikaika Kalikiano; ʻAhahui ʻŌpiopio ʻImi Pono o Karisto (old name).

youngster

Keiki, ʻōpio, ʻohā.

your

1. Singular possessed object, singular. Kou (o-class); kāu (a-class); kō (neutral class).

2. Singular possessed object, dual. Kō ʻolua (o-class); kā ʻolua (a-class).

3. Singular possessed object, plural. Kō ʻoukou (o-class); kā ʻoukou (a-class).

4. Plural possessed objects, singular. Ou (o-class); āu (a-class).

5. Plural possessed objects, dual. O ʻolua (o-class); a ʻolua (a-class).

6. Plural possessed objects, plural. Ō ʻoukou (o-class); a ʻoukou (a-class). For other uses of k-less (zero-type) possessives, see Gram. 8.4.2.

yours

Nou, no ʻolua, no ʻoukou, nāu, na ʻolua, na ʻoukou. See your.

yourself

ʻOe iho, ʻoe ponoʻī. See bone.

youth

ʻŌpio, ʻōpiopio, uʻi, ohi, ʻouo; liko (fig.). See sayings, lana 1, wini 1, morning. Time of youth, wā ʻōpiopio, wā uʻi, wā heuʻole, ʻehu kakahiaka.

youthful

ʻŌpiopio, uʻi, ʻouo.
